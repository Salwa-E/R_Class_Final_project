Author

Salwa Elsaadawy

Published

October 5, 2025

1 Introduction

This short report analyzes the built‑in mtcars dataset in R. The goal is to explore the relationship between fuel efficiency (miles per gallon, mpg) and engine/vehicle characteristics (e.g., weight, horsepower, transmission). I compute summary statistics, create visualizations, run a few statistical tests, and fit a simple linear regression model. Plain‑language takeaways are included after each result.

2 Data & Methods

  mutate(
    cyl = factor(cyl),

Notes on variables

  • mpg: miles per gallon (fuel efficiency, higher is better)
  • wt: car weight (1000 lbs)
  • hp: gross horsepower
  • cyl: number of cylinders (factor)
  • am: transmission (Automatic/Manual; factor)
  • Other variables: displacement (disp), rear axle ratio (drat), etc.

4 Visual Exploration

df %>%
  ggplot(aes(x = mpg)) +
  geom_histogram(bins = 10, alpha = 0.8) +

Takeaway: MPG is roughly unimodal with a tail toward higher efficiency.

df %>%
  ggplot(aes(x = cyl, y = mpg, fill = cyl)) +
  geom_boxplot(alpha = 0.8) +
  labs(x = "Cylinders", y = "MPG", title = "MPG by Cylinder Count")

Takeaway: Cars with more cylinders tend to be less fuel‑efficient (lower mpg).

df %>%
  ggplot(aes(x = wt, y = mpg, color = am)) +
  geom_point(size = 2, alpha = 0.9) +
  geom_smooth(method = "lm", se = TRUE) +
  labs(x = "Weight (1000 lbs)", y = "MPG", color = "Transmission",
       title = "Heavier cars get worse fuel economy; manuals tend to perform better")

Takeaway: Heavier cars have substantially lower mpg; manual cars often achieve higher mpg at similar weights.

8 Conclusion

  • Heavier and more powerful cars generally have worse fuel economy.
  • Manual transmission cars, on average, achieve higher mpg than automatics in this dataset.
  • A simple model with weight, horsepower, and transmission explains a substantial share of mpg variability, with weight being the dominant factor.

These results illustrate standard exploratory data analysis and basic modeling in R using Quarto. Because mtcars is an older, small dataset, findings are illustrative rather than definitive.
